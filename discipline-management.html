Discipline and Management

Geita Adventist School Discipline and Management

Discipline Management

When we hear the word discipline, what often comes into our mind is punishment. This is entirelya wrong perception of discipline. The word discipline is from Latin word “displina” means instruction or training. In other words it means to learn.

Geita Adventist schools are proud of how discipline is handled. Learners are taught to be obedient. According to Ellen G. White, one of the first lessons a child needs to learn is the lesson of obedience.

A child should be taught how to obey. is what Geita Adventist schools practice. In fact, we help learners to see that all things are under the Law and that disobedience leads in the end, to disaster and suffering. Also, we teach learners not only to be obedient to parents, teachers and other guardians but also to be obedient to the Most High God.

On top of the above, there are school rules and regulations that a learner is supposed to adhere to. The rules and regulations are clearly stipulated in the joining instruction.

Disobedience may ultimately lead to suspension or expulsion after constant guidance and counseling.

Spiritual Matters

Welcome to Religious and Spiritual Life at Geita Adventist Secondary School.

goal is to facilitate religious life in all its many forms and to provide a safe place for students and others to ask questions about meaning and purpose in life, often in relation to a supreme being, the world, and others around us.


Education at Geita Adventist Secondary School is not just about mastering subjects and getting good examination grades.

Beyond these academic principles are the Biblical truths that stand as the foundation for the Geita Adventist Secondary School education.

While striving for academic excellence, Christianity is integrated into all instruction on our schools.


It is the conviction of Geita Adventist Secondary School that the Bible in its entirety is the inerrant, inspired and powerful word of God, which explains, offers and gives salvation through faith in Jesus Christ and is the sole authority for faith and life. It is also our hope that all students will have this same conviction as their own.


In order to encourage and strengthen this conviction, Geita Adventist Secondary School provides a number of spiritual life services to which all students are invited.

Chapel Service

<

All students attend our daily morning devotion at parade. The service is held at 7:00 a.m. each day that classes are in session. During this ten-minute service, Geita Adventist Secondary School community gathers in worship to learn from the teachings and events of the Bible, sing hymns, and pray together.

Vespers Service

Everyday evening from 5:30 – 6 p.m. during the academic year, a Vespers Service is held in the chapel. This service provides a nice break from studies and a time of spiritual refreshment during the middle of the week.

Bible Studies

Bible studies are conducted at regularly scheduled times and locations announced by the Chaplain. These provide students with opportunities to hear, discuss, and raise questions about the teachings of God’s Word

Christ in the classroom

To promote spiritual life on campus, Geita Adventist Secondary School offers a number of religion teachings, daily chapel services, mid-week Vespers, informal Bible studies, and Christian student organizations. Students are encouraged to make the most of these opportunities so that when they leave Geita Adventist Secondary School, they will be equipped with a sound moral base, a sense of purpose, and an awareness of what is truly important in life.

Christ in media

Geita Adventist Secondary School’s School Choir promotes godliness, love, and unity focused on employing God’s gift of mass media to bring the pure Gospel message to vast audiences both in the Tanzania and abroad. The choirs proclaim His good news in song.

With Go we excel

As a Christian institution, Geita Adventist Secondary Schoolplace utmost importance on spiritual affairs. It is the belief of the school that “With God We Excel” – it is the foundation on which Geita Adventist School stands.

Religious life and wellness

Religious and Spiritual life works together with our larger school community to promote the health of the whole person. The spiritually well person seeks to use one's deeper understanding about purpose and meaning to live a life in which actions are consistent with one's beliefs, and to make the most of every day experiences with people and nature.
